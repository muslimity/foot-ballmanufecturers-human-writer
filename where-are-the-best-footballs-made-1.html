Under Armour

While not every football company uses the same manufacturing methods, Under Armour uses a combination of aerospace technology and machinery. They also use machines called “benders” that spin the raw materials into a perfect round shape. The machines are so accurate that Under Armour has their footballs measured for weight and inflation.

Nike

While the ball that is chosen to be used on the field is not produced by Nike, they do have a hand in the creation of the actual ball. The Nike Merlin ball is made using a process called “thermal molding”. The synthetic rubber is heated to the right temperature and then is placed in a machine with thousands of tiny cavities. The ball is then cooled and placed in a press machine that gives it its perfectly round shape. Finally, the ball is inflated and placed into packaging.

Wilson

Wilson is one of the oldest brands in the game, and all of their footballs are still produced in the United States. The company’s headquarters is located in St. Louis, Missouri. Wilson’s footballs are created in a certified production facility right here in the Midwest, which helps ensure consistency and quality.

Conclusion

The best footballs are made in China. The first footballs were made in China around the year 2000 AD when the game began to take off in the United States. Footballs were very different from the balls used today. In the beginning, the game was primarily played with a ball made of animal bladders or animal skins. Over time, people realized that the ball made of animal bladders was too heavy and flew straight forward instead of back. To solve this problem, people began using lighter materials to make the ball. England was one of the first countries to use synthetic materials to make the footballs.
